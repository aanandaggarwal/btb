Map of My Studio

Black and Gold Mackie Studio Monitors

These studio monitors provide excellent sound quality for mixing and mastering. I find them indispensable for achieving the right mix in my productions. They have a powerful, clear output that helps in identifying subtle nuances in the mix. I would recommend them for smaller studios, but also encourage buyers to consider low-end muddiness and room acoustics.

Universal Audio Volt 2

The Universal Audio Volt 2 is a cornerstone for my recording sessions, providing pristine audio quality with minimal latency. Great for home studios and those starting out, but watch out for its gain levels to avoid clipping.

Novation FLKey 37

This keyboard integrates seamlessly with FL Studio, making it a fantastic choice for FL Studio users. The touch response and integration features speed up my workflow significantly. I personally love using the onboard buttons to help with the dubbing process.

Akai MPC One Retro Edition

The Akai MPC One is a beat-making powerhouse. Its retro look and feel inspire creativity. It's highly recommended for anyone into beat production, though it does come with a steep learning curve. You can read more in my review article.

Rode NT-1A

The Rode NT-1A is my go-to microphone for vocals due to its crystal-clear sound and low noise. Perfect for recording vocals across genres, I'd dub this a must-have for any home studio, provided you have a good pop filter.

Logitech Wave K350 Keyboard

This ergonomic keyboard helps me avoid strain during long studio sessions. Its design and key response are good, though it might not be the best choice for those preferring mechanical keyboards. It's definitely not essential, but I included it because I love it.

FL Studio 21

Having used FL Studio for years, I've grown to appreciate its unmatched Piano Roll and efficient Step Sequencer which greatly enhance my production workflow. While FL Studio 21 continues to impress with features like stem separation, it can be resource-intensive and slow, which may not suit those with less powerful setups. For an alternative with wider compatibility and efficiency, consider Ableton Live or Logic Pro.

Behind the Beats: Visual Essay

By: Aanand Aggarwal

Welcome to the space where I get to escape the stress of life through music production. This visual journey will cover creating a song from scratch in my studio. From laying down the first note to tweaking the final mix, let's dive into my day-to-day of crafting hip-hop and R&B tracks.

Initial Setup: Tuning Into Creativity

The calm before the storm: my studio, ready for the sesh's creativity.

Every productive session begins with setting the right atmosphere. The process is almost ritualisticâ€”adjusting the lights, selecting the first reference/inspo track to break the silence, and ensuring all gear is connected and running. My routine allows me to more easily enter a flow state of focused creativity.

The Beat Begins

The starting point of many tracks: my record collection.

The foundation of any track starts with a solid beat. Using the Akai MPC, I sample old records, chop up beats, and lay down the initial rhythm that will define the track. Oftentimes, it's much easier to find the seed of an idea/feeling/vibe from the most unlikely places. I love picking one of my obscure $1 records at random, scratching through grooves, and chopping loops with the pads on the MPC One.

My MPC One takes in samples from my turntable, which I can then manipulate and sequence.

Adding melodies with the Novation FLKey 37.

With a bassline, pulse, or some other rhythm in place, I start to layer melodies and harmonies where they feel needed. I pick different virtual instruments/synthesizers to achieve dynamic, melodic, and rhythmic variation across the instrumental. The Novation FLKey 37 is my tool of choice, as it integrates directly with FL Studio to make MIDI recording much easier.

Making the Mix: Layering and Texturing

Working with a combination of my studio monitors and headphones, I work to refine the mix. The mixer in FL Studio 21 has an intuitive layout that easily allows me to review track routing, effect automation, and mixer inserts. One of my favorite quality-of-life hacks is to save preset racks with plug-ins for effects chains; for example, my "Strings" preset file will automatically load all plug-ins in my typical string instrument processing chain, set to my custom presets for quick fine-tuning.

Vocal Magic: Recording and Editing

I highly recommend the Rode NT-1A; a strong budget-friendly choice for musicians.

Writing, editing, and recording vocals is definitely the hardest part of the creative process for me. Especially since my musical journey began as an instrumentalist and producer, I find myself being overly critical of my lyricism and delivery. In a genre like hip-hop, it's important to remain true to yourself and confident in your work; thankfully, my Rode picks up a crystal clear signal and allows me to process my vocal to my heart's content.

The Volt 2 audio interface helps keep my mic signal clean, strong, and monitored.

Final Review: Listening and Tweaking

Track review in the studio: a time for critical listening and final adjustments.

As the late evening sets in, I take a break from producing and come back for some critical listening. This stage is all about fine-tuning, making minor adjustments to ensure the track plays well across different listening platforms. It is definitely a meticulous process, where I'll often upload multiple mixes to my iCloud Drive to test across devices, but every small tweak contributes to the track's overall quality. Besides, I wouldn't be satisfied finishing a track without polishing it to the best of my ability, so doing my due diligence here can help my song stand stronger.

While I typically invite a friend or two over to join in the process and/or record with me, the journey of today's studio session is typical yet unique. Each day offers new opportunities to evolve and there are always technical and creative challenges along the way, testing my determination and confidence as a musician. However, conquering these challenges brings a unique excitement and fuels a collaborative fire to create. I am eternally grateful for a space like my studio, equipped with tools like the Akai MPC, Novation keyboard, and Rode mic that let me express myself through such an amazing medium.

Farewell and until next time, from me and my pup!
